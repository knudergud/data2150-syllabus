Course Description

The introduction of widely available and accessible generative Artificial Intelligence tools, such as ChatGPT, democratizes expertise, unlocks knowledge, and bestows impressive abilities. This hands-on course provides students with practical experience employing generative AI to perform real-world tasks.

By the end of the course, students will be able to effectively collect accurate historical and real-time information, generate high-quality text and media, transform content between formats, analyze data to derive insights, and deploy generative AI to tackle private and professional challenges.

No pre- or co-requisites.

Use of AI in This Course

As this is a course on how to use AI tools, the use of such tools is expected and encouraged, as is the discussion of which tools were used, how they were used, and how they performed.

Goals & Learning Objectives

Course Goals

  1. Develop a comprehensive understanding of the principles, capabilities, and limitations of AI.
  2. Master the use of key AI tools and best practices for effective AI interaction and collaboration.
  3. Gain practical experience applying AI to real-world problems across various domains.
  4. Cultivate critical thinking skills for evaluating AI models, tools, and outputs.
  5. Explore emerging trends in AI and their potential impact on future professional landscapes.

Upon Completion, Students Will Be Able To:

  1. Explain and discuss the principles, capabilities, and limitations of generative AI.
  2. Master the use of key AI tools following best practices.
  3. Apply AI to real-world problems across various domains.
  4. Critically evaluate new AI models, tools, and outputs.
  5. Analyze trends in AI development and evaluate their potential impacts.

Evaluation & Grading

This course is designed around experiential learning — students learn through active engagement with hands-on AI projects — and low-stakes in-class assessment with frequent assignments that individually have a relatively low impact on the final grade.

Assignment Descriptions

"In-Class" Assignments (30%): Practical assignments tied to modules and topics, designed to provide hands-on experience with generative AI applied to real-world tasks. Detailed instructions provided in Canvas.

Static Website Project (15%, Individual): Each student uses AI tools (e.g., Claude Code, ChatGPT Codex, Perplexity Computer) to build a personal website.

App Project (15%, Individual): Each student uses AI (e.g., Google AI Studio) to build an app.

Interactive Website Project (20%, Team): Each team uses AI to build an interactive website.

Participation (10%): Active and meaningful engagement on Canvas — discussions, reflections, resource sharing, and a representative profile with name and photo.

Peer Assessment (10%): Confidential feedback on team members based on your experience working together.

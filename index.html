Course Description

The introduction of widely available and accessible generative Artificial Intelligence tools, such as ChatGPT, democratizes expertise, unlocks knowledge, and bestows impressive abilities. This hands-on course provides students with practical experience employing generative AI to perform real-world tasks. By the end of the course, students will be able to effectively collect accurate historical and real-time information, generate high-quality text and media, transform content between formats, analyze data to derive insights, and deploy generative AI to tackle private and professional challenges. There are no pre- or co-requisites.

Use of AI in This Course

As this is a course on how to use AI tools, the use of such tools is expected and encouraged, as is the discussion of which tools were used, how they were used, and how they performed.

Goals & Learning Objectives

Course Goals

  1. Develop a comprehensive understanding of the principles, capabilities, and limitations of AI.
  2. Master the use of key AI tools and best practices for effective AI interaction and collaboration.
  3. Gain practical experience applying AI to real-world problems across various domains.
  4. Cultivate critical thinking skills for evaluating AI models, tools, and outputs.
  5. Explore emerging trends in AI and their potential impact on future professional landscapes.

Upon Completion, Students Will Be Able To:

  1. Explain and discuss the principles, capabilities, and limitations of generative AI.
  2. Master the use of key AI tools following best practices.
  3. Apply AI to real-world problems across various domains.
  4. Critically evaluate new AI models, tools, and outputs.
  5. Analyze trends in AI development and evaluate their potential impacts.

Evaluation & Grading

This course is designed based on the principles of experiential learning and low-stakes in-class assessment.

Experiential learning is a method where students learn through active engagement and reflection on that experience. This includes working on hands-on projects that provide real-world experience with generative AI concepts and techniques.

Low-stakes "in-class" assessment involves frequent assignments that individually have a relatively low impact on the final grade, and are designed to reinforce the material covered in pre-class assignments and in-class activities.

Students will be evaluated individually and through a team project.

Assignment Descriptions

"In-Class" Assignments (30%): "In-class" assignments form an integral part of this course. These assignments are designed to provide students with practical experience in applying generative AI to real-world tasks while consolidating the information and skills gained from each class meeting. Assignments will typically involve the use of generative AI tools to perform tasks related to the learning objectives for the current topic. As there are no actual class meetings, these assignments are tied to modules and topics.

Static Website Project (15%, Individual): Each student will use AI to build a personal website using tools such as Claude Code, ChatGPT Codex, or Perplexity Computer. A detailed assignment will be provided in Canvas.

App Project (15%, Individual): Each student will use AI to build an app using tools such as Google AI Studio. A detailed assignment will be provided in Canvas.

Interactive Website Project (20%, Team): Each team will use AI to build an interactive website. A detailed assignment will be provided in Canvas.

Participation (10%): Participation is not merely about attendance but about being an active, engaged, and contributing member of the class. It represents a commitment to the learning community, where students share their insights, knowledge, tools, and time, enhancing the collective learning experience.

  • Active Engagement: Regular and meaningful interaction on Canvas, including participating in online discussion and reflection, and engaging with others.
  • Representative Profile: Students must use representative names and profile pictures to allow others to connect their names and faces.
  • Resource Sharing: Contributing to the class by sharing relevant information, resources, tools, and insights that align with the course content and enhance the learning experience.

Peer Assessment (10%): The peer assessment is a critical aspect of teamwork and collaboration within this course. It allows students to provide confidential feedback on their fellow team members based on their experience working together.
